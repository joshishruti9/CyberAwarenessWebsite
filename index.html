Cyber Security Awareness

Stay Safe Online

Cyber security is essential in today’s digital world. Follow these tips to protect yourself:

  • Use strong and unique passwords.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Keep software and systems updated.
  • Use secure Wi-Fi connections.
  • Never share personal details, OTPs, or passwords over calls or emails.
  • Verify suspicious calls claiming to be from banks or tech support.

Recognizing Phishing Emails

Phishing emails attempt to trick you into revealing personal information. Follow these tips to recognize them:

  • Check the sender's email address for inconsistencies.
  • Be cautious of unexpected links or attachments.
  • Look for spelling errors and generic greetings like “Dear User.”
  • Beware of urgent or threatening language pressuring immediate action.
  • Verify suspicious emails by contacting the sender through a trusted method.

Creating a Strong Password

A strong password helps protect your online accounts from hackers. Follow these guidelines:

  • Use at least 12 characters.
  • Include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Avoid using your name, birthdate, or common words.
  • Use unique passwords for different accounts.
  • Consider using a password manager to store your passwords securely.
  • Change passwords periodically and never reuse old passwords.

Enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security to your accounts. Here’s how to enable it:

  • Step 1: Log in to your account and go to Security Settings.
  • Step 2: Look for an option like Two-Factor Authentication or Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Step 3: Choose your preferred MFA method:
    • Text Message (SMS): Receive a code on your phone.
    • Authenticator App: Use apps like Google Authenticator or Authy.
    • Email: Receive a code via email.
    • Hardware Key: Use a physical device like a YubiKey.
  • Step 4: Follow the setup instructions (e.g., scan a QR code for an authenticator app).
  • Step 5: Save backup codes in a secure place in case you lose access to your MFA method.
  • Step 6: Test the MFA process to ensure it works.

Tip: Enable MFA on all critical accounts like email, banking, and social media.

Congratulations! You are now a warrior against cyber attacks!
